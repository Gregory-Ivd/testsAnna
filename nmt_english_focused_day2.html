Частина 3. Reading — Matching Headings

Підберіть заголовок (A–F) до кожного абзацу. Один заголовок зайвий. (Q15–Q19)

Headings:
A) Start with what you already love
B) Every day, even briefly
C) Speak from day one
D) Why grammar isn't the whole story
E) The myth of the perfect age
F) Learning alone is never enough

Paragraph 15. Many adults believe they are simply "too old" to pick up a new language. Yet research repeatedly shows that motivation, time invested, and quality of practice matter far more than age. A 50-year-old who studies consistently can easily outperform a teenager who studies once a month.
Paragraph 16. The biggest mistake most beginners make is waiting until they "know enough" before opening their mouth. The truth is the opposite: speaking is what makes the language stick. Even a clumsy conversation teaches you more than another hour of silent reading.
Paragraph 17. Twenty minutes a day will take you further than three hours once a week. Languages reward consistency, not heroic bursts of effort. Build study into your morning coffee or your evening commute — somewhere it will not vanish on a busy day.
Paragraph 18. Rules of grammar matter, but they are not the heart of a language. You can know every conjugation and still freeze when a waiter asks what you want. Real confidence comes from use, not from charts in a textbook.
Paragraph 19. Choose materials that connect to your existing interests. If you love football, watch matches with commentary in your target language. If you enjoy cooking, follow recipes in the original. Engagement, not difficulty, is what drives real progress.
